Deep Class-specific Affinity-Guided Convolutional Network for Multimodal Unpaired Image SegmentationPaper

Multi-modal medical image segmentation plays an essential role in clinical diagnosis and remains challenging as single modalities are often not well spatially aligned. Existing learning-based methods mainly consider sharing trainable layers across modalities and minimising visual feature discrepancies.While the problem is often formulated as joint supervised feature learning, multiple-scale features and task-specific representation has not yet been explored. In this paper, we propose an affinity-guided fully convolutional network for multimodal image segmentation. To learn effective representations, we design class-specific affinity matrices to encode the knowledge of hierarchical feature reasoning, together with the shared convolutional layers to ensure the cross-modality generalisation. Our affinity matrix does not depend on spatial alignments of the visual features and thus allows us to train with unpaired, multimodal inputs. We extensively evaluated our method on two public multimodal benchmark datasets and outperform state-of-the-art methods。

Adversarial Convolutional Networks with Weak Domain-Transfer for Multi-Sequence Cardiac MR Images Segmentation CodePaper

Analysis and modeling of the ventricles and myocardium are essential in the diagnostic and treatment management of heart diseases. Manual delineation of those tissues in cardiac MR (CMR) scans is laborious and time-consuming. The ambiguity of the boundaries makes the segmentation process rather challenging. Furthermore, the annotations of some modalities, such as Late Gadolinium Enhancement (LGE) are often not available. We propose an end-to-end segmentation framework based on a convolutional neural network (CNN) and adversarial learning. The dilated residual U-shape network was used as a segmentor to generate the prediction mask; meanwhile, a CNN was utilized as a discriminator model to judge the segmentation quality. To leverage the available annotations across modalities per patient, a new loss function named \emph{weak domain-transfer loss} is introduced to the pipeline. The proposed model was evaluated on the public dataset released by the challenge organizer in MICCAI 2019, which consists of 45 sets of multi-sequence CMR images. We demonstrate that the proposed adversarial pipeline outperforms baseline deep-learning methods.

Counterfeit Anomaly using GAN for Anomaly Detection Paper

We propose a novel framework called adGAN for anomaly detection using generative adversarial networks (GAN). adGAN is a discriminative model, which is learned only from normal samples for anomaly detection. The design of adGAN considered two key elements 1) a fake pool generated by a standard Wassterstein GAN using only normal samples, and 2) a critic with a new loss function, which is trained by taking fake pool as abnormal samples. The experiments on grey-scale abnormal digits detection, RGB skin cancer detection and MR brain tumour detection showed that adGAN is consistently superior to its competitors, including the well-known one-class SVM.
